ORCCAD - Project Overview

Mission and Scope

What is the purpose and scope of this document ?

This document aims at presenting a general description of the ORCCAD V4 software. It describes, in a very general way, the major objectives of the software and the major constraints of development and exploitation. It is addressed to users and software framework builders.

What is the scope of this project?

ORCCAD is a methodology and a software environment dedicated to the design and the implementation of advanced robotics control systems. It also allows the specification and the validation of missions. ORCCAD is mainly oriented towards critical real-time applications in robotics. Here, automatic control issues are strongly connected with discrete-event aspects. Such systems are called hybrid systems.

In this class of applications, ORCCAD is particularly concerned with systems which strongly interact with their environment, through several actuators and sensors. The control architecture of such systems is generally on board, and the criticality of the application is emphasized by the very high direct or indirect cost associated with a failure: since it is difficult or even impossible to physically intervene on a long range automatic underwater vehicle, or on an autonomous rover working on a contaminated site, it is necessary to minimize a priori the risk of non achievement of a mission. For that purpose, ORCCAD provides the user with dedicated interfaces ensuring the safety of programming, and has capabilities of validation, either by formal verification or by extensive simulation.

More details on this software will be found on the website of the Project http://orccad.gforge.inria.fr.

What are the main objectives and motivations ?

ORCCAD is about 15 years old project and since 1998, the software ORCCAD V3 was not be upgraded to a major release. The main objective of ORCCAD V4 is to completly rewrite the software keeping the basic ideas (ORCCAD approaches).
Besides the standard features which are required for illustrating the ORCCAD approaches, the objectives of this project are of the following :
  • To be open to current methods and tools of controller modelization (Scilab/Scicos, Matlab/Simulink, UML,...)
  • To support exchanges and comparisons of methods between researchers. In particular, in the distributed and real-time robot controller domain.
  • To disseminate the know-how to other fields of research and industry.
  • To take into account the diversity of users (end users, experts, software developers, framework builders, industrial users, etc ... ).
  • To ensure software quality by the use of modern software design methods.

What is the exploitation context ?

Compared to other systems dedicated to this class of applications, ORCCAD offers a coherent and full solution, from the high level specification to the generation of real-time code. It can also been customized to fulfill the requirements of a particular problem. The tool is open to general purpose tools (Scilab/Scicos, Matlab/Simulink, UML,...).

Through dedicated interfaces, and in relation with software tools adapted to every design level, ORCCAD can be used:

  • by an engineer or a scientist in charge of launching the system in view of a given application: for instance, he can describe the mission of an underwater observation robot in terms of combination of elementary behaviors associated with recovery procedures; macro simulations and formal verifications can also be performed from the resulting specification;
  • by an expert in automatic control who has to design the elementary actions, to implement and tune the feedback loops and to specify the event-driven behavior of these actions; validation tools can also be connected at this level; besides, tool boxes like predefined control schemes or robot models are available;
  • by a computer scientist, expert in real-time issues, who defines the target architecture and the related communication and execution aspects; code generators and simulation tools will allow him to verify the validity of his technological choices.

The ORCCAD software should be used as well by research academic teams as by industry. Currently, ORCCAD V3 is used in three ways :

  • Research for Pop-Art to experiment :
    • distributed and real-time robot controller
    • validation of missions
  • Robots controller (Biped robot) for Sed.
  • Demonstrators for Trasys Space :
    • project XXX to test controller for a ESA Mars Rover
    • project XXX of visual servoing

With the ORCCAD V4, we expect to replace the ORCCAD V3 use and to adress new research academic teams.

Status

The ORCCAD V3 is obsolete and is not distributed due to license problems. ORCCAD V4 is currently under specification, a first public version will be ready for October 2006.
